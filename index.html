Maqsudur Rahman

Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science, Boise State University, USA · Expected Ph.D. Completion: May 2027

Former Assistant Professor, Lecturer, and Senior Lecturer in Computer Science / ICT

Hello, I am Maqsudur. My research lies at the intersection of trustworthy artificial intelligence, hybrid quantum-classical computing, medical image analysis, and secure machine learning. I design compact and reliable quantum-enhanced learning models that remain effective under realistic data imperfections, including noisy labels, corrupted imaging inputs, weak supervision, quantum trainability challenges, and training-time backdoor poisoning.

Before starting my Ph.D. at Boise State University in August 2024, I served as an Assistant Professor at Comilla University and previously as Lecturer/Senior Lecturer at Comilla University and Port City International University. My teaching experience covers programming, data structures, algorithms, operating systems, information security, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and deep learning. I have supervised undergraduate projects and thesis work and remain committed to student-centered, project-based computer science education.

Current Dissertation Direction

My dissertation investigates robust hybrid quantum-classical learning for trustworthy medical image classification under data imperfections and security threats. The work studies quantum circuits as compact representation modules rather than generic classifier replacements, with emphasis on stability, prototype-guided geometry, trustworthy decision support, and secure learning under noisy or poisoned training environments.

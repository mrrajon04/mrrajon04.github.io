Profile

My research lies at the intersection of trustworthy artificial intelligence, hybrid quantum-classical computing, medical image analysis, and secure machine learning. I design compact and reliable quantum-enhanced learning models that remain effective under realistic data imperfections, including noisy labels, corrupted imaging inputs, backdoor poisoning, weak supervision, and quantum trainability challenges.

My broader research goal is to develop trustworthy, interpretable, and data-efficient AI systems for safety-critical domains, particularly medical and biomedical decision support.

Current Research Direction

During my Ph.D., I have developed a coherent research program in robust quantum machine learning. My recent work includes NQNN for noisy-label medical image classification, QAPNet for robust patchwise classification under corrupted inputs, and HQADNet, an under-review framework for secure medical AI under training-time backdoor poisoning.

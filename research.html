Research Summary

My research develops robust hybrid quantum-classical learning methods for trustworthy clinical AI. I study how variational quantum circuits, prototype-guided learning, uncertainty-aware training, and secure representation learning can improve reliability under noisy labels, corrupted inputs, weak supervision, and backdoor poisoning.

Major Research Threads

NQNN: Noise-aware Quantum Neural Networks

NQNN addresses noisy-label medical image classification by integrating Fourier attenuation, uncertainty-based sample reweighting, and adaptive pooling within a variational quantum circuit framework.

QAPNet: Quantum-Attentive Patchwise Network

QAPNet addresses corrupted imaging inputs and weak supervision using sliding-window patch extraction, quantum patch encoding, additive-attention multiple-instance aggregation, contrastive alignment, and prototype regularization.

HQADNet: Backdoor Poisoning Defense

HQADNet, currently under review, studies secure and adversarially robust medical AI through EM-refined soft prototypes, trust-aware training, and an 8-qubit tree-structured quantum encoder for training-time backdoor poisoning defense.

Systems-Level Hybrid Quantum-Classical Learning

My systems-oriented research studies barren plateau mitigation and practical support for hybrid quantum-classical medical imaging pipelines.
